Curious about people, technology, media, design

I’m a PhD candidate in Information Science seeking the next step in my career as a human-computer interaction researcher.

My research explores how to bring data into our everyday lives as a creative material for understanding ourselves and the world around us. I take a design-centric approach, focusing on data representations that are participatory and engage all of our senses, especially sound. I’m also researching how we might reconsider voice assistants as tools for creativity.

I’ve also done projects on how people access news and information across various technologies and social settings, how we can use data that streaming services log about us as a design resource, how people listen to podcasts, and how crafting and exchanging personal artifacts can be a collaborative activity for making sense of data we track about ourselves.

In the past, I've worked as a data journalist covering the energy industry and as a data liaison helping non-profit organizations understand their communities through data.

As a competitive trail runner, I love ultra-distance races, relays, and multi-day adventures. I grew up in Oregon, am fascinated and terrified by mountain lions, and frequently geek out over podcasts, board games, and sour beer.
